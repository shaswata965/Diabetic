Over The Counter (OTC) Medications

OTC medications are Over-The-Counter drugs. These are medications that you can purchase from a pharmacy without a doctor's prescription. On sick days, avoid OTC medications that can raise your blood glucose levels. Read the labels to find out if they contain “phenylephrine”or “pseudoephedrine”. These ingredients can raise your blood glucose as well as your blood pressure.

OTC medications to avoid include Cough syrups, cough drops/lozenges that contain sugar, Sudafed®, and Tylenol® Cold products.
