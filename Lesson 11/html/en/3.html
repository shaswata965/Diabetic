Sick day action plan: Monitor Ketone Levels

Ketones are chemicals appearing in your body when you don't have enough insulin to turn sugar into energy.

Your body will use fat for energy source. The liver turns this fat into ketones.

When you have diabetes, you can build up too many ketones in your blood and it can become life-threatening- condition called Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA).

The symptoms of DKA include:

  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Abdominal pain
  • Fruity-smelling breath
  • Fast labored breathing
  • Change in the level of consciousness

To monitor your ketone at home:

  • You will need urine testing strnps, Note that test strips do expire.

  • To use the testing strips, dip the testing end of the strip into your urine. Wait as instructed on the label (usually about 15 seconds).

  • Compare the color of the test strip with the color chart on the product label

  • If the color change is equal to a moderate or large amount of ketones, call your provider.
