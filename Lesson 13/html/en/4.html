Urine albumin to creatinine ratio (UACR) and blood creatinine test

Having diabetes increases your risk for diabetic kidney disease.

If you have high levels of blood glucose for a long time, it can slowly damage your kidneys.

You may not even know this is happening. The UACR is one of the tests used to monitor your kidney health.

A UACR of 30 to 299 mg/g may indicate early signs of kidney disease if you have type 1 diabetes and an increased risk if you have type 2 diabetes.

Important

If you have type 1 diabetes, you should begin testing for kidney disease 5 years after diagnosis, then once every year.
If you have type 2 diabetes, you should test when you are diagnosed, and then once yearly thereafter.
