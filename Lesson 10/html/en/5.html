Tips for Exercising
  • Warm up before exercise and cool down after, if needed
  • Wear socks that absorb sweat during physical activity to avoid blisters and other foot injuries.
  • Coordinate food, medication, and physical activity. For moderate activity that lasts 30 minutes to 1 hour, you should consider a pre-activity snack with 15 g of carbs. Pre-activity snacks are meant to prevent your blood glucose level from dropping too low.
  • Drink water before, after and during physical activity. Drink more water in hot and humid climates because sweating leads to loss of body water.
  • Use safe areas to exercise. If you will be alone, tell someone where you will be
  • Ways to assess effort.
    • Talk test: If you can talk, you are training at moderate effort; if you can talk in short phrases you are working at vigorous effort; if you cannot talk, you are training too hard
    • Measure your heart rate if told to do so by your healthcare provider
