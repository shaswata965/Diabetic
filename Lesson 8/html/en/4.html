How to Mix Different Insulins?

Steps for mixing clear and cloudy insulin:

  • Wash your hands and clean your injection site

  • Pick up the CLOUDY bottle and roll it gently to mix the insulin

  • Use alcohol to clean the rubber top of the vial

  • Pull the plunger down to the correct unit mark for your CLOUDY insulin dose

  • Insert the needle into the CLOUDY bottle and push the plunger down to inject air

  • Withdraw the empty syringe from the bottle. Set the bottle aside

  • Pull the plunger down to the correct unit for the CLEAR insulin dose

  • Insert the needle into the CLEAR bottle and push the plunger down to inject air

  • Turn the bottle upside down with the needle in it

  • Pull the plunger down to the correct unit for the CLEAR insulin dose

  • Look for air bubbles in the syringe. If you see any repeat

  • Turn the CLOUDY bottle upside down and push the needle into it

  • Pull the plunger down and withdraw the units for the CLOUDY insulin

  • The plunger should now be on the unit mark showing the total units of both the CLEAR and CLOUDY insulins

  • Look for air bubbles in the syringe

  • Pinch the skin and insert the needle straight into the skin. Make sure the needle is all the way in

  • Push the plunger down to inject the insulin. Count to five

  • Pull the needle out and discard it as advised.
