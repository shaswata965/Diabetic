Retinopathy (Eye disease)

Retinopathy occurs when thereâ€™s damage to the small blood vessels in the eye that can lead to vision problems.

Risk

Diabetic retinopathy is estimated to be the most frequent cause of blindness among adults.

Prevention

  • Get a yearly eye exam
  • Adults with Type 1 diabetes need a dilated eye exam within five years after the onset of diabetes then annually.
  • Adults with Type 2 diabetes need an eye exam shortly after diagnosis then annually.
  • Call your diabetes care provider if you notice changes in your vision.
  • Keep blood glucose levels at target
  • Check blood pressure at target
  • Quit smoking
  • Discuss your physical activity with your eye doctor.
  • Women who have retinopathy should avoid taking birth control pills.
