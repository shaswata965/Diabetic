Oral Medications

  • Metformin is the generic name for Glucophage, Glucophage XR
  • Commonly the first-line agent for the treatment of type 2 diabetes

How it works?

  • It reduces the amount of glucose released by your liver.
  • Metformin also helps your body cells (fat and muscle) use insulin better.

How to take it?

  • Your doctor will start with a low dose and then increase it gradually.
  • Do not crush the pill.
  • Prescribed once or twice a day.
  • It should be taken with the meal.

Side effects

  • Gastrointestinal effects- metallic taste, abdominal bloating, nausea, diarrhea, cramping
  • Slight weight loss

Gliclazide is the generic name for Diamicron. Glipizide for Minidiab, Glimepiride for Amaryl and Glibenclamide for Daonil.

How it works?

  • Sulfonylureas stimulate your pancreas to produce more insulin. Having more in your system allows your body to better regulate your glucose levels.

Side effects

  • hypoglycemia, which has symptoms like sweating, dizziness, confusion.

Pioglitazone is the generic name for Actos

How it works?

  • Pioglitazone work by helping your body cells use insulin better.
  • If taken in combinations with other diabetes medicines, there may be a risk for hypoglycemia.

How to take it?

  • Usually prescribed once daily, with or without food.

Side effects

  • Weight Gain
  • Swelling in the feet or lower legs, shortness of breath, trouble breathing at night
  • Muscle Aches, abdominal pain

Sitagliptin is the generic name for Januvia and vildagliptin is the generic name for Galvus.

How it works?

  • It blocks the action of an enzyme so that it cannot destroy the GLP-1 hormone produced.
  • This GLP-1 hormone stimulates your pancreas to produce insulin after a meal. The enzyme helps lower the amount of glucose released by the liver after a meal.

How to take it?

  • It comes in a pill form, taken with or without food
  • It could be found as combination with Metformin, called Janumet®

Side effects

  • Gastrointestinal effects like diarrhea or abdominal pain, headache and runny nose are rare but may occur
  • Canagliflozin is the generic name for Invokane.
  • Dapagliflozin is the generic name for Forxige.
  • Empagliflozin is the generic name for Jardiance.

How it works?

  • It blocks the reabsorption of glucose in the kidney. They work by increasing the amount of insulin released from your pancreas.
  • This increases the excretion of glucose in the urine and decreases blood glucose levels.

How to take it?

  • It comes in pill form and should be taken in the morning as it increases urination frequency.

Side effects

  • Increased risk of urinary tract, genital, and upper respiratory tract infections.
  • Repaglinide is the generic name for Novonorm.
  • Nateglinide is the generic name for Starlix.

How it works?

  • It increases the amount of insulin released from pancreas. If pancreas can no longer produce insulin, these medicines will not be effective. They work by increasing the amount of insulin released from your pancreas.
  • Checking your pre- and post-meal glucose levels will show you its effectiveness.

How to take it?

  • It comes in a pill form. Take within 30 minutes of eating meal. If you skip a meal, do not take the medicine.

Side effects

  • Hypoglycemia is possible
  • Acarbose is the generic name for Glucobay 50.

How it works?

  • This blunts the rise in blood glucose after meals. Checking the blood glucose 2 hours after meals will show whether acarbose is effective.
  • Acarbose work by limiting the absorption of carbohydrates in your intestine.

How to take it?

  • This pill is taken with the first bite of food.
  • If hypoglycemia occurs while taking acarbose, treatment should be with glucose tablets or glucose gel only!

Side effects

  • Gastrointestinal effects like flatulence, diarrhea and abdominal pain are the main side effects of acarbose.
