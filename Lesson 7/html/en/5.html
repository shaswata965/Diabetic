Insulin Injectables

There are many types of insulin, and they work in different ways.

Insulin types are classified according to their onset (when it starts to work), peak (maximum effect), and duration (how long it stays in body).

For safety reasons, it's important to know how your insulin works!

Rapid acting insulin:

Taken before meals usually, to control the blood glucose rise caused by food.Onset time: 5-15 minutes.

    Examples: NovorapidⓇ, HumalogⓇ

Short acting/Regular Insulin:

Taken 30 minutes before meals, to control the blood glucose rise after a meal but with a lag in its action.
Onset time: 30- 60 minutes

    Examples: ActrapidⓇ

Intermediate Insulin:

It is intended to impact the blood glucose rise many hours after injection.Onset time: 1-2 hours.
Duration time: 10-18 hours Thoroughly mix this solution before injecting.

    Examples: HumulinⓇ

Long acting Insulin:

Long acting basal Insulin works in the background for up to 24 hours. Some newly developed long-acting insulins work up to 30-42 hours, like TresibaⓇ and ToujeoⓇ.

    Examples: LantusⓇ, ToujeoⓇ, LevemirⓇ, TresibaⓇ
