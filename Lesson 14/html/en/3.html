Coping with a New Diagnosis

Everybody responds differently to diabetes.

After your diagnosis you may feel:

  • Denial/Shock

    A first response can be a shock.
    Denial is a way of protecting yourself.

  • Anger/Resentment

    YNormal when knowing that diabetes is a lifetime illness, not going away…

  • Guilt

    You may feel guilty thinking that you caused diabetes to yourself.

  • Worried or Depressed

    People often worry about what to expect.

How to cope with these feelings:
  • Talk to someone close to you about how you are feeling.
  • Focus on the positives. You are in the driver’s seat and have the power to not let diabetes completely affect your life.
  • Talk to your provider if feelings of depression or hopelessness are prolonged.
Remember: these are all normal experiences.

Your thoughts and feelings will change over time.
