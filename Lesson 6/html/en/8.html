Food Label

Food labels provide valuable information for healthy eating. Don’t worry, all the facts and information can be easy to understand with these tips

  • Look at the serving size on the Nutrition Fact label and compare it with what you will eat.
  • Check which nutrients are high or low in a serving of food by looking at the % on the label.
  • The most important part of the label is the amount of carbohydrates in 1 serving. Aim for consistent carbohydrate target per meal.

The list of Ingredients shows ingredients listed by weight. The first one is what you have the most of!

Droobi Tip

Try replacing sugar with low-calorie sweeteners (saccharin, aspartame, acesulfame K, sucralose, stevia…) that have no effect on blood glucose levels. Try different kinds of sweetener for taste or mix it with sugar
