The Macronutrients

The energy in our food comes from 3 main nutrients:

Protein:

It helps the body build, repair, and maintain most of your body's tissues and organs. It includes all meat, beans and dairy product.

Fat:

It provides the body energy, keeps skin healthy, protects body organs, helps body absorb nutrients and produce hormones. There are different types of fats:

  • Unsaturated Fat- can improve heart health, found in fish, nuts, avocados and vegetables oils.
  • Saturated Fat- can increase risk for heart disease, found in animal products, butter, fatty meat cuts...
  • Trans fats- increase risk for heart disease, mostly found in processed foods.

Too much fat or too much of the wrong type of fat can be unhealthy.

Protein and fat do not get broken down into glucose in the blood. It slows down the digestion of sugar and starch.

Carbohydrate:

Its main function is to provide your body with energy. Most of the carbohydrates in are broken down into glucose. There are 3 types of Carbohydrates in food:

  • Sugars: fruits, milk, yogurt, honey, white sugar… Turns into glucose very quickly
  • Starches: grains, legumes, starchy vegetables… Turns into glucose more slowly
  • S Fiber: nuts, seeds, vegetables, fruits, whole grains, beans… Doesn’t turn into glucose,it slows down the breakdown of carbohydrates, prevent peaks in blood glucose levels.

Droobi Tip

Increase fiber in your diet, as it helps to lower blood glucose and cholesterol levels, as well as helping with keeping you feeling full!
