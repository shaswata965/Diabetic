MyPlate Food Groups: Fruits and Dairy

Fruits are loaded with vitamins, minerals and fiber, but they contain natural sugar. Be mindful with portions. Aim for whole fruits as a healthy snack. Limit dried fruits and juices, as they increase blood sugars if eaten in large amounts.

Dairy products are a great source of calcium and high-quality protein. While it is important to have it as part of the meal plan, choose low fat cheese, yogurt and milk (skim or 1%).

Droobi Tip

When drinking karak or other hot drinks, like lattes and cappuccinos), avoid adding condensed milk, because it’s high in sugar. Choose low fat milk. Also, avoid the the fruit-flavored, sweetened yogurts, even if they have the word “Lite” on them –even “lite” yogurts can have added sugars.
