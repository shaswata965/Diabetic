Hypoglycemia

Acute complications are usually short-lived, such as hyperglycemia and hypoglycemia.

Hypoglycemia is when your blood glucose level drops to a level that is below 70 mg/dl.

Symptoms include:

  • Shakiness
  • Dizziness
  • Sweating
  • Anxiety or nervousness
  • Trouble concentrating
  • Intense hunger
  • Headache
  • Blurred vision
  • Fast pulse/heart skips a beat

When people get frequent hypoglycemia, they can have “hypoglycemia unawareness”; they don’t feel symptoms the same way anymore! Scary!

Reasons include:

  • Delaying or skipping meals
  • Taking wrong dose of medication or at a wrong time
  • Unusual intense physical activity
  • Alcohol consumption

Nocturnal Hypoglycemia

Is when your blood sugar levels drop below normal during your sleep. Common symptoms include:
