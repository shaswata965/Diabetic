Glucagon Injection

If left untreated, hypoglycemia may lead to a seizure or unconsciousness. In this case, someone else must take over.You can call the ambulance at 999 and inject glucagon quickly.

Injectable glucagon, available by prescription, is used as a medication to treat severe hypoglycemic events. It is usually administered by a family member.

How to Administer Glucagon:

  1. Inject glucagon into the individual's buttock, arm or thigh, following the manufacturer's instructions.
  2. When the individual regains consciousness (usually in 5-15 minutes), they may experience nausea and vomiting. Turn them to their side to prevent choking.
  3. Discuss this event with your healthcare provider to plan hypoglycemia prevention.

Do NOT:

  • Inject insulin (will lower blood glucose even more)
  • Provide food or fluids (individual can choke)
  • Put hands in mouth (individual can choke)
